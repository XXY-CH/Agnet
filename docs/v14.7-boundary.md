# v14.7 Boundary — Policy and Risk Routing Signals

状态：complete
协议标签：`v14.7-protocol`

## 目标

v14.7 extends the verifier-owned `FED_QUERY` discovery ranking surface with two inspectable routing signals: `policy_match` and `risk_match`. The goal is to let a local verifier prefer workers whose existing descriptor policy fits the query task scope, and whose already-verifiable trust/revocation/reputation evidence indicates lower execution risk.

```text
FED_QUERY capability + intent + optional scope
  ↓
Verifier-owned discovery ranking
  policy fit / credential trust / revocation state / receipt reputation
  ↓
FED_QUERY_RESULT discovery_evidence.routing + ranking.reasons
```

## v14.7 新增

- Node and Go `queryMatch` include `policy_match` in `discovery_evidence.routing`.
- `policy_match` is derived only from existing worker descriptor `policy` and query/task `scope` fields already enforced by policy checks, including network and write-prefix allowances.
- Missing policy-scope constraints fall back to neutral score `5` and do not increment `signals_used`.
- Matching policy constraints score higher; policy denials score lower.
- Node and Go `queryMatch` include `risk_match` in `discovery_evidence.routing`.
- `risk_match` is derived only from existing capability credential trust/active state, signed local authority revocation state, and completed-receipt reputation evidence.
- Missing risk evidence falls back to neutral score `5` and does not increment `signals_used`.
- `agent_score.total` includes `policy_match` and `risk_match` alongside the existing receipt, credential, freshness, cost, latency, availability, and revocation components.
- `ranking.reasons` can include `policy_match` and `risk_match` when those verifier-owned signals positively contribute.
- Existing deterministic tie-breaks remain score descending, then worker alias ascending.

## v14.7 不新增

- Not a new trust oracle.
- Not a new policy language.
- Not remote attestation, sandbox proof upgrade, or global risk scoring.
- Not a marketplace ranking graph or remote reputation feed.
- Not opaque ML routing.
- Not a bypass around `enforcePolicy`; execution policy checks remain authoritative.

## 为什么这是 v14.7

v14.2 exposed cost, latency, and availability as transparent routing signals. v14.7 keeps the same pattern but adds two verifier-owned signals that were already implicit in discovery safety: whether the worker policy can satisfy the query scope, and whether local credential/revocation/reputation evidence makes the match lower risk.

## 验收

- Node `queryMatch` exposes integer `policy_match` and `risk_match` under `discovery_evidence.routing`.
- Go `queryMatch` mirrors the Node routing signal shape.
- `agent_score.total` includes both new signals with neutral fallbacks for missing evidence.
- `ranking.reasons` includes `policy_match` and `risk_match` for positive verifier-owned signal matches.
- Docs-contract tests assert `v14.7-protocol`, `policy_match`, `risk_match`, and this boundary document while preserving v14.6 Knowledge Gateway boundary coverage.
